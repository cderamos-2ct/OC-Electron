# CTO Weekly Brief Template

## Purpose
Provide a repeatable weekly brief that turns Christian's CTO learning and operating work into something concise, useful, and shareable.

This brief should help answer:
- what did I learn this week?
- what changed in my understanding?
- what matters strategically?
- what should we test next?
- what should partners or teams know?

---

## 1) Executive Snapshot
- **Week of:**
- **Primary focus:**
- **Current thesis:**
- **Why it matters now:**

## 2) What I Learned
List the most important things learned this week.

Format:
- insight
- why it matters
- where it applies (ServFlow / Visual Graphx / group / client work)

## 3) What Changed
What changed in understanding, assumptions, or direction?

Examples:
- architecture understanding changed
- market understanding changed
- priority changed
- new risk/opportunity emerged

## 4) Source Highlights
What sources mattered most this week?

For each:
- source title
- format (internal doc / article / video / audio / repo / paper)
- why it was worth the time

## 5) Open Questions
What still needs to be answered?

Keep this tight.
These are not random curiosities.
These are the questions that would unlock better decisions.

## 6) Recommended Experiments
What should the dev team or leadership test/explore next?

For each:
- idea
- why now
- expected upside
- risk/uncertainty
- smallest useful test

## 7) Partner / Leadership Takeaways
What should partners or leadership know from this week?

Keep this section concise and high-signal.

## 8) Team Takeaways
What should the team know or act on?

This can include:
- technical direction
- experiment guidance
- architecture implications
- execution notes

## 9) Next Week Focus
- what to keep pushing
- what to review next
- what to drop/ignore

---

## Usage modes

### Private version
Use for full candid thinking.
Can include sharper observations, hypotheses, and unresolved ideas.

### Shareable version
Trim to:
- executive snapshot
- key learnings
- recommended experiments
- leadership/team takeaways

---

## Rule
The weekly brief should not be a diary.
It should be a leverage document.
